Case Study

Optimising network investment under real-world constraints.

Supporting a large-scale infrastructure operator with more consistent investment decisions across cost, demand, sequencing, and delivery constraints.

← Back to case studies

Context

A major expansion programme required better decision structure.

The client was planning a multi-region network expansion involving substantial capital commitments, sequencing decisions, and delivery constraints.

Outcomes were shaped by both demand uncertainty and operational feasibility. Existing planning approaches relied on static assumptions and simplified models.

This limited the ability to compare scenarios consistently, understand trade-offs clearly, and connect strategic priorities to execution realities.

Problem

The challenge was not data availability, but decision quality.

Capital and phasing

Investment decisions needed to reflect budget limits, phasing constraints, and competing priorities across regions.

Demand uncertainty

Regional demand assumptions varied materially, affecting expected returns and sequencing.

Delivery constraints

Operational feasibility, timing, and execution constraints needed to be incorporated into planning decisions.

Consistency

Existing tools did not support a clear, repeatable way to compare options on a like-for-like basis.

Approach

A decision system linking planning logic, constraints, and scenario analysis.

Structure

Explicitly framed the decision to capture constraints, trade-offs, sequencing choices, and alternative investment paths.

Model

Integrated demand assumptions, planning logic, and investment evaluation to support realistic scenario comparison.

Deploy

Built tools that allowed stakeholders to explore scenarios dynamically in live decision-making.

Result

Stronger consistency, clearer trade-offs, and better planning decisions.

Trade-off visibility

Better visibility into how cost, demand, timing, and delivery constraints affected investment choices.

Decision consistency

More structured evaluation across regions, reducing dependence on fragmented analysis.

Scenario speed

Faster and more systematic comparison of alternative planning options and build sequences.

Execution alignment

Stronger alignment between strategic priorities, planning choices, and delivery realities.
