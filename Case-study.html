Case study

Optimising network investment under real-world constraints.

Supporting a large-scale infrastructure operator with more structured, consistent planning decisions across cost, demand, and delivery constraints.

Context

A major expansion programme required better decision structure.

The client was planning a significant network expansion across multiple regions. The decisions involved substantial capital commitments, sequencing choices, and delivery constraints, with outcomes shaped by both demand uncertainty and operational feasibility.

Existing planning approaches relied on static assumptions and simplified models. This limited the ability to compare scenarios consistently, understand trade-offs clearly, and connect strategic priorities to execution realities.

Problem

The challenge was not data availability, but decision quality.

The client had access to relevant data, domain knowledge, and planning inputs. The real gap was the absence of a structured, repeatable way to evaluate investment options under real-world constraints.

Capital and phasing

Investment decisions needed to reflect budget limits, phasing constraints, and competing priorities across regions.

Demand uncertainty

Regional demand assumptions varied materially, affecting expected returns and the sequencing of expansion.

Delivery constraints

Operational feasibility, timing, and execution constraints needed to be incorporated into planning decisions.

Consistency

Existing tools did not support a clear, repeatable approach to comparing scenarios on a like-for-like basis.

Approach

A decision system linking planning logic, constraints, and scenario analysis.

We designed and built a framework that combined scenario evaluation, optimisation logic, and planning inputs in a single system usable in live decision-making.

Structure the problem

Explicitly framed the decision to capture constraints, trade-offs, sequencing choices, and alternative investment paths.

Model the system

Integrated demand assumptions, planning logic, and investment evaluation to support more realistic scenario comparison.

Support live use

Built tools that allowed stakeholders to explore scenarios dynamically, with usability and operational relevance in mind.

Result

Stronger consistency, clearer trade-offs, and better planning decisions.

The new system improved the quality and consistency of investment decisions by making trade-offs explicit and scenarios easier to compare.

Trade-off visibility

Better visibility into how cost, demand, timing, and delivery constraints affected investment choices.

Decision consistency

More structured evaluation across regions, reducing dependence on fragmented analysis and one-off assumptions.

Scenario speed

Faster and more systematic comparison of alternative planning options and build sequences.

Execution alignment

Stronger alignment between strategic priorities, planning choices, and delivery realities.

The framework now supports core planning and investment decisions, improving both transparency and repeatability in a high-stakes environment.
