Case Study

Optimising network investment under real-world constraints.

A large-scale infrastructure operator required a more robust approach to planning network expansion—balancing cost, demand, and operational constraints.

Context

The client was planning a significant network expansion programme across multiple regions. Existing approaches relied on static assumptions and simplified models, limiting the ability to evaluate trade-offs across cost, demand, and delivery constraints.

Decision-making was fragmented, with limited visibility into how different assumptions impacted overall investment outcomes.

Problem

The core challenge was not data availability, but decision structure. The client needed a way to consistently evaluate multiple scenarios under real-world constraints, including:

  • Capital limitations and budget phasing
  • Demand uncertainty across regions
  • Operational and delivery constraints
  • Competing investment priorities

Existing tools were not designed to handle this level of complexity in a structured, repeatable way.

Approach

We designed and built a decision system that integrated data, constraints, and optimisation logic into a single framework.

  • Structured the decision problem to explicitly capture constraints and trade-offs
  • Developed optimisation models to evaluate investment scenarios
  • Integrated demand modelling with network planning logic
  • Built tools to allow stakeholders to explore scenarios dynamically

The focus was not just model performance, but usability—ensuring the system could be used in live decision-making.

Result

The new system significantly improved the quality and consistency of investment decisions.

  • Improved visibility into trade-offs across cost, demand, and risk
  • More consistent decision-making across regions
  • Ability to evaluate multiple scenarios quickly and systematically
  • Better alignment between strategy and execution

The system is now used as a core tool in planning and investment decision processes.
