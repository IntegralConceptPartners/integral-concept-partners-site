Case study

Optimising network investment under real-world constraints.

Supporting a large-scale infrastructure operator with more structured, consistent planning decisions across cost, demand, and delivery constraints.

Context

A significant network expansion programme required better decision structure.

The client was planning a major network expansion across multiple regions. Existing approaches relied on static assumptions and simplified models, limiting the ability to evaluate trade-offs across cost, demand, and delivery constraints.

Decision-making was fragmented, with limited visibility into how different assumptions affected investment outcomes and delivery priorities.

Problem

The challenge was not data availability, but decision structure.

The client needed a way to evaluate multiple scenarios consistently under real-world constraints, rather than relying on isolated analysis and simplified planning tools.

Capital and phasing

Investment decisions needed to reflect budget constraints, phasing, and competing priorities.

Demand uncertainty

Regional demand assumptions varied materially, affecting both sequencing and expected returns.

Operational constraints

Delivery feasibility, timing, and execution constraints had to be incorporated into decisions.

Consistency

Existing tools did not support a structured, repeatable way to compare scenarios.

Approach

A decision system linking data, constraints, and optimisation logic.

We designed and built a framework that combined scenario analysis, optimisation, and planning logic in a single system usable in live decision-making.

Structure

Explicitly framed the decision problem to capture constraints, trade-offs, and sequencing choices.

Model

Integrated demand modelling with network planning logic and investment evaluation.

Use

Built tools that allowed stakeholders to explore scenarios dynamically, with usability in mind.

Result

Improved consistency, transparency, and decision quality.

Trade-off visibility

Better visibility into cost, demand, and risk trade-offs across scenarios.

Decision consistency

More structured evaluation across regions and investment choices.

Scenario speed

Faster and more systematic comparison of alternative planning options.

Execution alignment

Stronger alignment between strategic intent and operational delivery.

The system now supports core planning and investment decision processes.
