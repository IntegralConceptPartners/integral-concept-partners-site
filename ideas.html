This month’s recommendations.

A short list of books, podcasts, and articles shaping how we think about decisions, systems, and complex environments.

March selection

Three things we think are worth your time.

Kept deliberately short: one book, one podcast, and one article or essay.

Book

Thinking in Systems — Donella Meadows

A concise, enduring introduction to systems thinking. Still one of the clearest ways to understand feedback loops, constraints, and why well-intentioned interventions often fail.

Podcast

Invest Like the Best

One of the better long-form interview podcasts for understanding how serious operators and investors think about markets, incentives, capital allocation, and asymmetric opportunities.

Article

The Business of Expertise

A useful reminder that expertise only creates value when it can be translated into judgment, decisions, and action. Particularly relevant for firms working at the intersection of analysis and execution.
