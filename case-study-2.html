Case study

Improving capital allocation under uncertainty.

Helping an infrastructure investor make more consistent portfolio investment decisions across risk, return, sequencing, and delivery constraints.

← Back to case studies

Context

Portfolio-level investment decisions required a more consistent decision framework.

The client was evaluating investment opportunities across a fragmented portfolio of network assets. Decisions required balancing expected returns with delivery risk, capital constraints, and market uncertainty.

Existing approaches relied heavily on static models and qualitative judgement, limiting comparability across opportunities and reducing visibility into downside risk.

Problem

The challenge was to move from isolated deal evaluation to structured portfolio decision-making.

The client needed a consistent and repeatable way to evaluate investment choices under uncertainty, and to compare opportunities on a like-for-like basis across the portfolio.

Assumptions

Investment cases were built on inconsistent assumptions, limiting comparability across opportunities.

Risk visibility

There was limited transparency into downside risk and exposure across the portfolio.

Prioritisation

No systematic way existed to prioritise and sequence capital deployment decisions.

Scalability

Existing approaches did not support structured evaluation at portfolio scale.

Approach

A structured decision framework linking financial logic, operational constraints, and scenario analysis.

We developed a decision framework that improved consistency, transparency, and comparability across investment opportunities, while supporting portfolio-level decision-making.

Standardise

Created a consistent basis for evaluating opportunities across the portfolio.

Integrate

Linked demand, cost, and delivery models to reflect real-world constraints in investment evaluation.

Optimise

Enabled scenario-based analysis and portfolio-level prioritisation of capital deployment.

Result

Clearer trade-offs, stronger comparability, and improved portfolio control.

Return vs risk

Clearer visibility into trade-offs between upside, downside risk, and delivery constraints.

Consistency

More structured and comparable evaluation across investment opportunities.

Prioritisation

Improved sequencing and prioritisation of capital deployment decisions across the portfolio.

Strategic control

Better alignment between portfolio strategy and execution realities.

The framework now supports ongoing investment decisions and portfolio management, improving transparency, repeatability, and confidence in capital allocation.
