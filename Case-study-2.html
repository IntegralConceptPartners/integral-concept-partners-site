Case Study

Improving capital allocation under uncertainty.

An infrastructure investor required a more structured approach to evaluating investment opportunities across a portfolio of network assets.

Context

The client was evaluating multiple investment opportunities across a fragmented portfolio. Decisions required balancing expected returns with delivery risk, capital constraints, and market uncertainty.

Existing approaches relied heavily on static models and qualitative judgement, limiting consistency across opportunities.

Problem

The challenge was to move from isolated deal evaluation to a structured, portfolio-level decision framework. Key issues included:

  • Inconsistent assumptions across opportunities
  • Limited ability to compare investments on a like-for-like basis
  • Unclear visibility into downside risk
  • No systematic way to prioritise capital deployment

The client needed a way to evaluate opportunities dynamically, under uncertainty, and at scale.

Approach

We developed a structured decision framework linking financial modelling, operational constraints, and scenario analysis.

  • Standardised assumptions across investment cases
  • Integrated demand, cost, and delivery models
  • Built scenario-based evaluation tools
  • Enabled portfolio-level optimisation of capital allocation

The focus was on improving decision consistency and transparency—not just model accuracy.

Result

The new approach significantly improved the quality and comparability of investment decisions across the portfolio.

  • Clearer visibility into return vs risk trade-offs
  • More consistent evaluation across opportunities
  • Improved prioritisation of capital deployment
  • Better alignment between investment strategy and execution

The framework is now used to support ongoing investment decisions and portfolio management.
